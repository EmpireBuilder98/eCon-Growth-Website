Section 02
Get Past
The Gate.
CSRs and dispatchers are not the enemy. They're the door. And every door opens with the right key.
The rule
Sound like you belong. Be warm. Be brief. Be confident. Do NOT sound like a salesperson — sound like someone who already has a relationship and is just following up.
OPEN
First Contact
▸ You say
"Hey, good morning — is [owner name] around? This is Kristopher with eCon Growth."
⚡ Note
Use their name if you researched it. If not, say "the owner" — not "the manager." Owners respond to being addressed as owners.
IF
"What's this regarding?"
▸ You say
"Yeah, totally. I work with HVAC companies in [city] — we put in AI systems that handle incoming calls and booking automatically. I just want to see if it makes sense for them. It'll be quick."
⚡ Note
Do NOT say "software," "CRM," "platform," or "sales call." Keep it conversational. "AI system that handles calls" sounds interesting, not threatening. Curiosity is your friend.
IF
"He's busy / unavailable"
▸ You say
"No problem at all. What's usually the best time to catch them — morning or afternoon?"
◆ Them
"Probably mornings before 9."
▸ You say
"Perfect. I'll call back then. And hey — what's your name? I'll ask for you directly so I'm not a complete stranger next time."
⚡ Note
Getting the CSR's name is gold. Use it every single callback. "Hey Sarah, it's Kristopher again" changes the dynamic from cold call to familiar relationship instantly.
PIVOT
"We're slammed right now"
▸ You say
"Absolutely. Actually — I was thinking about swinging by with coffee and donuts for the crew one morning anyway. Would [owner name] have 15 minutes to chat while everyone grabs a bite? I just want to show them something real quick in person."
⚡ Note
This is the coffee pivot. Use it whenever they put you off. It's generous, disarming, and nearly impossible to say no to. If they say yes — lock the date immediately. Do not leave without a day.
REFRAME
"We already have software"
▸ You say
"That's actually fine — most of the guys we work with do. This isn't really a software thing. It's more about — are your phones getting answered when nobody's available? And is someone following up on leads at midnight? That's where Roger comes in. Worth 15 minutes to show [owner name] what it actually does."
⚡ Note
Never argue about their software. Never compete with it. Reframe to the gap their software leaves — phones unanswered, no overnight follow-up. Every HVAC company has this problem regardless of their stack.
TRANSFER
When they connect you
▸ You say
"Thank you so much — I really appreciate it. Can I get your name? I want to make sure I tell [owner] you took care of me."
⚡ Note
Edifying the gatekeeper to the owner creates instant credibility before you say hello. The owner hears "Sarah took care of him" and you're already a different kind of caller.

Tip 1 — Research before you dial
Look them up. Google their reviews. Check their website. Know how many trucks they're running, what neighborhoods they serve, what their Google rating is. Walk into every call knowing one specific thing about their business. "I saw you guys serve the Jordan Valley area — how competitive is it out there?" That one sentence separates you from every other cold caller they've ever heard.
Tip 2 — Pain first. Always.
Do not start with what Roger does. Start with what they're losing. Ask about their phones. Ask about their dispatch when volume spikes. Ask about their close rate on inbound calls. Let them surface the wound. Once you know where it hurts — you're not selling anything. You're offering a cure.
Tip 3 — The coffee and donuts play works every time
I mean it. Every time. It's impossible to be rude to someone who just fed your crew. It pre-loads generosity into the conversation before you've said a single word about the product. Use it as your pivot whenever they try to put you off. Not later — now. Lock a morning before you hang up.
Tip 4 — Callback dates are non-negotiable
Every conversation that doesn't end in a booked visit MUST end with a callback date. "When's a good time to circle back?" and then you set the date right there. If they say "whenever" — you say "how about Thursday morning?" Don't leave the conversation open-ended. An open end is a dead lead.
Tip 5 — Call Roger in front of everyone
Don't describe what Roger does. Don't show a slide. Dial (385) 349-4337, put it on speaker, and let them hear it. I've watched that call change a room in 90 seconds. Owners go quiet. Their dispatcher leans in. The conversation shifts from "what is this" to "how do we get this." That call IS the pitch. Use it every time.
Tip 6 — "We're selective" is your most powerful exit
When they're lukewarm, don't chase. Say it with total confidence: "We're selective about who we work with — no rush on my end." Then mean it. Walk away clean. Half the time they call back within 48 hours. The moment you stop needing the sale is the moment they start wanting the product.
Tip 7 — Three touches minimum before you give up
Phone, text, phone. That's the floor. Most HVAC owners are running on adrenaline — they missed your first two touches because they were under a crawlspace. The third touch is often the one that lands. Log every contact in the tracker. Set the callback date. Work the Follow-Up Engine every single morning before you dial new numbers.
Tip 8 — Know your numbers and own them
If they push back on price, don't flinch. "Foundation is $1,499 a month. Let's figure out if we need to even have that conversation first — show me what one missed call is costing you and we'll do the math together." Confidence in your price signals confidence in your product. If you're unsure, they're unsure. Lock in.
